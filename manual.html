ReceptionOperating guide

How to run this, simply.

You do four things: get clients, set them up, keep them happy, collect money. The software answers their customers in seconds, day and night, out loud and in writing, and sends you every enquiry. This is everything you need to run it.

02Add a client (about 10 minutes)

  1. Open the owner console and log in.
  2. Type their business name, pick their trade, hit Create front desk.
  3. You get their customer link and QR and their admin link on screen.
  4. Open the admin link and fill in their real services, prices, hours, the About your business box, and pick a style (Minimal, Boutique, Bold, Sleek, Elegant, Noir or Soft) and their colour. Hit Save. (Or just send the admin link to the client and let them fill it in themselves.)
  5. Send a test lead from their admin to check their Telegram is connected (section 05).
  6. Give them their customer link and QR. Instagram bio, WhatsApp, shopfront, name cards. Live.
Trade not in the list? Pick the closest one and edit, or "General". There are 14 trade brains built in (beauty, home and trades, cleaning, tuition, fitness, F&B, pet, events, automotive, professional services, repairs, property, insurance, general). Any business works.

03How the front desk knows their business

It answers only from what is filled into the admin: their services, prices, hours, common questions, and the About your business box. The more that goes in, the sharper it sounds. It speaks every reply aloud as well as in text, in the client's own name and colours.

It is built to never make things up. If it does not know something, it does not guess, it takes the customer's name and number as an enquiry and tells them the business will follow up. For regulated trades (property, insurance) it shares only general information and always hands off to the licensed person.

04How enquiries reach you (and why none get lost)

When a customer leaves their name or number, typed in the chat or via the details button, it becomes an enquiry. Every enquiry is saved to the dashboard first, so it can never be lost, then sent onward. It shows up in:

  • The client's admin (their Leads list)
  • Your owner console (all leads across every client)
  • The client's Telegram, instantly (if connected)
The safety net: if an enquiry cannot reach a client's Telegram (not connected, or a glitch), it still saves, it shows a clear "not sent to your Telegram" flag in their admin, and you get an alert on your Telegram so you can nudge them to fix it. Nothing slips through quietly.

05Connect a client's Telegram (do this at setup)

This is the one step people forget, so do it with them:

  1. The client opens Telegram and messages @receptionbot once. It replies with their chat ID (a number).
  2. They paste that number into their admin, under "Where your enquiries go", and Save.
  3. Hit Send test lead in the admin. If a test message lands in their Telegram, it is connected.

Until this is done, enquiries still appear in the dashboards, and you will get the "not delivered" alerts, so you will know to chase it.

06Changing things later

You or the client open the admin anytime, change a price, add a service, update hours or the About box, hit Save, and it is live on the very next customer. Nothing to rebuild, no waiting.

07Money and costs

  • Front desk: S$88 a month per client. Custom AI solutions: quoted.
  • You collect payment yourself for now (PayNow, bank transfer, or an invoice). No billing is built in yet.
  • Your running cost is tiny, a fraction of a cent per reply, a few dollars a month across everything. The margin is yours.
  • Each front desk has a generous daily chat limit (600 a day) as an abuse guard. You get a Telegram heads-up at 80% and at the cap, and you can raise any client's limit in the console. Real small businesses never come close.

08Show a prospect (your best sales move)

Open the home page (reception.sg) on your phone in front of them, and swipe to the property desk. Let them talk to it, ask about a listing, watch it capture the lead and stay CEA-safe. Then say: "This is yours, in your name and colours, in a few minutes, for S$88 a month." That demo closes more than any pitch.

09Common tasks (all in the owner console)

  • Pause a client (stopped paying, on hold): their front desk shows a polite "not available" until you un-pause.
  • Delete a client: removes them and their leads for good.
  • Reset a client's admin link: makes a fresh key if the old link leaked. The old admin link stops working.
  • Raise a daily limit: for a client with genuine high demand.
  • See all leads across every client, in one place.

10If something looks wrong

  • "This front desk isn't available" → the client is paused, or the link is wrong.
  • Thin or vague answers → add more detail in the admin. The About box and common questions do the most work.
  • Client says no Telegram alerts → they missed section 05. Check the chat ID is saved and hit Send test lead. Their leads are safe in the dashboard meanwhile, and you would have had a "not delivered" alert.
  • Voice not playing on an iPhone → it needs one tap first (Apple rule). After the first tap on the page, it speaks.
  • Locked out of the console → it is your owner key; it can be reset.

11Under the hood (only if you are tinkering)

You do not need this to run the business, but for reference:

  • The sales site, admin and front desk are plain web pages hosted free on GitHub Pages (the adamdegoat/reception repo, served at reception.sg). Publishing a change is a git push.
  • The brain is one Cloudflare Worker called nimbus-desk. Every client is a row in its database (D1); the code is generic. Deploying a change is wrangler deploy from ~/nimbus-desk.
  • Replies use Claude (Haiku); voice uses a free text-to-speech; alerts use the Telegram bot. Secrets (the API keys, your owner key, your Telegram ID) live in the Worker, not in any page.
  • There are Privacy and Terms pages (linked in every footer) written for Singapore's PDPA. Point clients there if they ask.
  • Easiest path for any change: just tell Claude what you want and it edits and redeploys.

12What to tell every client about the law

Two things they are responsible for, not you. Say them at setup so nobody is surprised later.

The Do Not Call registry. The front desk hands them Singapore mobile numbers. Replying to someone who just messaged them is fine, that is answering an enquiry. But if they later send that number a marketing message, a new launch blast, a promo, a "just checking in" campaign, the PDPA's Do Not Call rules apply, and they must check the number against the DNC Registry first unless they hold clear and unambiguous consent in writing. Fines here are real and land on the agent, not on us. Tell them to check before any broadcast.

They are the data controller for their own leads. The enquiries belong to their business. If a customer asks them to delete their details, they action it, and they can clear their enquiries from the console at any time. Our privacy policy at reception.sg/privacy.html covers what we do as their service provider, including who processes the data and where.

13The one rule

Everything the front desk says comes from what is in the admin. Keep that filled in well, connect their Telegram, and each front desk runs itself while you go find the next client.

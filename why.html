Being first to reply wins the client.

This is not a gimmick. It is built on a simple, well-studied truth: whoever answers fastest, and never goes quiet after hours, is the one who wins the client. Here is the evidence, and why it holds for property, insurance and financial professionals.

Speed decides who gets the client.

When someone reaches out, they are usually asking a few agents or advisers at once. The one who replies first, and sounds on the ball, almost always wins. This is one of the most studied findings in sales.

21×

You are up to twenty-one times more likely to turn a new enquiry into a real client when you reply within five minutes instead of waiting thirty.

MIT lead-response study, Prof. James Oldroyd
7×

Reach a lead within the first hour and you are seven times more likely to have a real conversation, yet the average firm takes more than forty hours to reply.

Harvard Business Review, 2,241 companies

Reception replies in seconds, day or night, captures the lead and routes it straight to you.

A missed enquiry is a client signing with someone else.

Most enquiries do not wait. If nobody answers, people do not leave a note and hope, they simply message the next agent or adviser on their list.

8 in 10

People who reach a voicemail hang up without leaving a message. Most do not try again later, they move on to the next agent.

The details differ, but the rule is the same: whoever answers first, wins.

The buyer who can't reach you

A buyer sees your listing at 11pm and messages three agents. The first to reply gets the viewing. Now that's you, in seconds, budget and area captured, no figure ever quoted.

The seller thinking it over

A homeowner messages after dinner about selling. Answer instantly, book the valuation session without quoting a figure, and they list with you, not the next agent who replied quicker.

The insurance enquiry on a Sunday

Someone asks about a hospital plan for their parents on the weekend. It captures what they're after and books the consultation, so they meet you before another agent gets to them.

The late-night financial question

A prospect messages about retirement planning at midnight. It takes their details and books a consultation, without ever giving advice, so the lead is yours by morning.
